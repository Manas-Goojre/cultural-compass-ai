# Live Pitch Template (5-7 minutes)

Fill this in as soon as the problem statement drops and your plan solidifies. Rehearse it out loud, timed.

## 0:00 - 0:30 — Hook (open on the problem, in the audience's language)

"{One or two sentences: who has this problem, and why it's painful right now.}"

## 0:30 - 1:15 — What we built, in one breath

"{One sentence: what the product does and for whom.}"
Then go straight into the demo — don't linger on a slide.

## 1:15 - 4:15 — Live demo (the core of the pitch)

Click through the 2-3 things from `DEMO_SCRIPT.md`, narrating what's happening and why it matters, not what
buttons you're pressing. Show the happy path complete, start to finish, once, cleanly.

1. {Feature/step 1 — what the judge sees, why it matters}
2. {Feature/step 2}
3. {Feature/step 3, if time allows}

If live demo fails: "Let me show you the recording I captured earlier, and I'm happy to also try it live
after." Don't panic-debug on stage.

## 4:15 - 5:15 — How it's built (this is where "prompt quality/architectural elegance" gets judged directly)

"{One sentence on the architecture: e.g. 'React frontend, FastAPI backend, Gemini via Vertex AI for X.'}"
"{One sentence on your prompting approach: e.g. 'I planned the data model and flow first in Planning Mode,
built the happy path end-to-end before anything else, then used the agent's browser to self-verify before
polishing — so nothing on stage right now is something I haven't already watched work.'}"
"{One sentence on the single most deliberate AI/architecture decision you made and why.}"

## 5:15 - 6:15 — Impact / what's next

"{Who benefits, how much time/effort/money/risk this saves them, in concrete terms if possible.}"
"{One sentence on what you'd add with more time — shows judgment, not just a finished feature list.}"

## 6:15 - 6:45 — Close

"{One confident closing sentence. Thank the judges. Stop talking.}"

## Delivery notes

- Demo first, explain second — never open with a slide of bullet points.
- Say numbers if you have them (rows of data, seconds saved, % improvement) — concrete beats vague.
- Practice the transition INTO the demo and OUT of the demo — those are the moments people fumble.
- Time yourself twice tonight with a stopwatch on a placeholder pitch, so tomorrow it's just filling in
  blanks under pressure, not building pitch-speaking skill from zero.
